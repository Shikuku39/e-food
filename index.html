MENU:

This restaurant's menu boasts an impressive range of delicious Kenyan dishes that cater to all tastes and preferences. The menu is divided into several categories, including appetizers, entrees, desserts, and beverages. The appetizers feature some of the most popular Kenyan dishes, such as samosas, a savory pastry filled with spiced meat, vegetables, or cheese. The skewered mshikaki, made of marinated meat, and the refreshing kachumbari salad made with fresh tomatoes and onions, are also on offer. Sweet fried bread known as mahamri is also available to whet your appetite.
Moving on to the entrees, the menu offers an extensive selection of dishes that are sure to satisfy your hunger. Nyama Choma, a grilled or roasted meat dish, is a popular option that comes in various forms, such as beef, goat, or chicken. Pilau, a fragrant spiced rice dish served with either beef or chicken, is another tempting option. For those who prefer a vegetarian meal, there is a selection of dishes to choose from, including Sukuma Wiki, sautéed collard greens, and Irio, a mashed potato dish with green peas, corn, and onions. Lastly, Githeri, a hearty bean and corn stew, is a flavorful dish that is popular in Kenya.
The dessert options are equally enticing and will satisfy your sweet tooth. Mandazi, a sweet fried bread, is a classic Kenyan dessert, while Kaimati, sweet dumplings, and Mutura, a grilled sausage, provide a unique and delicious experience.
To complete the dining experience, the menu offers a range of refreshing beverages. Kenyan tea, a staple in Kenya, is a must-try. Spiced Masala chai is another popular beverage that is perfect for a chilly evening. Mango juice is a refreshing choice, and Tusker Beer, a popular Kenyan beer, is perfect for those who enjoy a cold brew with their meal. Overall, the restaurant's menu offers an impressive selection of Kenyan cuisine that is sure to leave your taste buds satisfied.

Appetizers:

  1. Samosas - Crispy pastry filled with spiced vegetables or meat.
  2. Mshikaki - Skewered meat, usually chicken or beef, marinated in a spicy sauce.
  3. Kachumbari - Tomato and onion salad with lemon and chili peppers.
  4. Mahamri - Sweet fried bread made with coconut milk and served with hot tea.

Entrees:

  1. Nyama Choma - Grilled or roasted meat, often goat or beef, served with ugali, kachumbari, and chili sauce.
  2. Pilau - Spiced rice dish made with cumin, cardamom, cinnamon, and cloves, served with beef or chicken.
  3. Githeri - Hearty bean and corn stew made with kidney beans and maize.
  4. Sukuma Wiki - Collard greens sautéed with onions and tomatoes, often served with ugali or rice.
  5. Irio - Mashed potato dish mixed with green peas, corn, and onions.

Desserts:

  1. Mandazi - Sweet fried bread made with flour, coconut milk, and sugar, often served with hot tea.
  2. Kaimati - Sweet dumplings made with flour, sugar, and cardamom, served with honey syrup.
  3. Mutura - Grilled sausage made with beef or goat meat, often spiced with coriander and chili peppers.

Beverages:

  1. Kenyan Tea - Strong, sweet tea made with milk and spices like ginger and cardamom.
  2. Tusker Beer - Popular Kenyan beer made from locally sourced ingredients.
  3. Mango Juice - Freshly squeezed juice made from sweet Kenyan mangoes.
  4. Masala Chai - Spiced tea made with milk, cinnamon, cardamom.
